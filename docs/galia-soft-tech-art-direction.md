# Galia Soft-Tech Art Direction

## Product idea

Every important character, creature, vehicle, weapon, and collectible should feel desirable as both a digital game asset and a physical toy. The visual test is simple: **would a child recognize it at thumbnail size and want to hug, collect, or display it?**

The system takes its tactile, character-first lesson from Claynosaurz and the merchandise clarity of Pudgy Penguins, while creating an original Guardians of Galia identity. It does not reproduce either reference's characters.

## Shape language

- Use compact 3–4-head-tall character proportions.
- Keep heads and hands slightly oversized, with rounded shoulders, boots, helmets, and armor corners.
- Preserve the source faction silhouette before adding cuteness.
- Replace sharp tactical forms with padded, pill-shaped, inflated, or chamfered forms.
- Make every major prop readable at 64 pixels with one dominant shape and no unnecessary surface noise.

## Faces and emotion

- Preserve the original character's face structure, species traits, hairstyle, markings, and age.
- Enlarge eyes modestly, never into infant or uncanny-doll proportions.
- Use soft cheeks, expressive brows, clear mouth shapes, and kind resting expressions.
- Bosses may be mischievous, grumpy, or imposing, but never graphic or horrifying.

## Materials

- Primary: dense minky plush, padded woven fabric, soft-touch vinyl, brushed felt.
- Secondary: satin stitching, embroidered insignia, rubberized sci-fi panels, softly glowing inserts.
- Metal should appear only as small, smooth accent pieces.
- Seams must support believable plush construction instead of becoming decorative noise.

## Faction translation

- **MUD:** charcoal fabric armor, warm red patches, practical pockets, rounded human explorer shapes.
- **ONI:** jewel-like color accents, elegant soft fins and crests, luminous embroidery, graceful silhouettes.
- **USTUR:** friendly modular robotics, large readable eye lights, rounded plates, magnetic-looking joints.
- **Ahr:** dark berry and violet forms, soft spikes, grumpy expressions, readable warning glows.

## Object translation

- Weapons become tools: chunky grips, short barrels, rounded muzzles, glowing status bands.
- Ships use toy-like massing, broad canopies, oversized thrusters, and simple faction color blocks.
- Crystals and pickups resemble translucent candy-like vinyl with soft inner light.
- UI portraits should use chest-up crops; gameplay pieces should prioritize silhouette over texture.

## Production rules

1. Keep the original licensed reference only as source material; save the cute reinterpretation as a separate versioned asset.
2. Never overwrite an original ArtStation download.
3. Produce a neutral character master before action poses or skins.
4. Validate each design at full size, portrait-card size, and 64-pixel gameplay size.
5. Prefer one memorable feature per character that can become a plush, keychain, sticker, or emote.
6. No copied franchise characters, logos, text, watermarks, gore, or realistic weapons aimed at the viewer.

## First approved benchmark

`nova-reyes-mud-pilot-v1.png` establishes the humanoid target: recognizable mature MUD pilot identity, compact proportions, padded black-and-red suit, tactile stitching, friendly expression, and a manufacturable full-body silhouette.

`puri-companion-v1.png` establishes the creature target: source-faithful alien anatomy, bean-shaped plush construction, embroidered ridges, glossy readable eyes, and a single greeting gesture that can become an emote or toy pose.
